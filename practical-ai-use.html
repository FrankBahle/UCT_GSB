Data and Dashboard Case Study

Explore synthetic customer data, analyse Mxit Mxit bundle performance and convert customer, service and commercial information into evidence-based executive decisions.

Synthetic Executive Training Case

TNM Mxit Mxit Bundle Performance and Customer Retention Analysis

From customer data to commercial decisions. Participants act as TNM's customer and commercial analytics unit and investigate sales, usage, customer experience, network quality and retention.

Case Overview

The Business Challenge

TNM requires a clearer, evidence-based view of Mxit Mxit bundle performance across Malawi. Management needs to understand which bundles and customer groups generate value, how network experience affects satisfaction, what drives repeat purchasing and which customers may become inactive or churn.

Core Management Question How can TNM use customer and service data to improve bundle revenue, customer experience and repeat purchasing while reducing inactivity and churn?

Case Information

01

Understand

02

Analyse

Investigate patterns in revenue, usage, customer experience, network quality and retention.

03

Visualise

Use the Excel and HTML dashboards to filter, compare and communicate important findings.

04

Recommend

Convert the evidence into practical commercial, customer, channel and network actions.

Investigation Questions

Analyse the evidence

Use the dashboard and downloadable data to answer the following management questions.

01

Product and Revenue

Which bundles generate the most purchases and total revenue?

02

Customer Demand

Which customer segments, regions and districts show the strongest demand?

03

Bundle Utilisation

How fully are customers using their data, voice and SMS allowances?

04

Network Experience

Does network generation, download speed or downtime affect satisfaction and retention?

05

Digital Channels

How do USSD, the TNM Smart App and payment methods compare?

06

Customer Retention

Which behaviours and service conditions are associated with inactivity, risk or churn?

Management Deliverable

Turn the dashboard into an executive recommendation

Working individually or in groups, prepare a concise management response based on the evidence.

Five Findings

Identify the five most important commercial, customer or operational findings.

Three to Five Actions

Recommend actions covering revenue, retention, customer experience, channels or network service.

Responsible Use

Explain the risks, assumptions, fairness concerns and human oversight required.

Data Ethics and Responsible Analytics

This is a fictional teaching case. Participants should minimise personal data, protect confidentiality, avoid discriminatory profiling and retain accountable human judgement when interpreting predictions and recommendations.
