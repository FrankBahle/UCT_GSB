# Facilitator Guide — AI Leadership in Action

**Prepared for:** Telekom Networks Malawi and Strategic Partners  
**Dates:** 15–17 July 2026  
**Venue:** UCT Graduate School of Business, Cape Town  
**Facilitator:** Professor Abejide Ade-Ibijola

## Facilitation Philosophy

- Begin with TNM's organisational reality.
- Position participants as executive co-designers.
- Make AI practical and visible through demonstrations.
- Diagnose pain points before designing solutions.
- Combine strategic discussion with co-creation and prototyping.
- Test concepts for value, feasibility, legality, explainability, inclusion and ethical risk.
- Move towards pilot-ready concepts, ownership and 90-day action.
- Connect programme impact to Malawi and Africa.

## Day 1 — Introductions, Shared Understanding and Pain-Point Diagnosis

**08:30–16:00**

Facilitate executive introductions, programme orientation, the AI Reality Game, applied demonstrations, the TNM and Malawi reality check, leadership perspectives, pain-point diagnosis, prioritisation and challenge-team formation.

**Output:** aligned expectations, validated realities, prioritised pain points and cross-functional teams.

## Day 2 — Co-Creation, Practical AI and Rapid Prototyping

**08:30–16:00**

Facilitate design criteria, use-case matching, practical AI demonstrations, solution architecture, rapid prototyping, walkthroughs and peer critique.

**Output:** AI-enabled concepts, tangible prototypes, assumptions, feedback and Day 3 governance questions.

## Day 3 — Strategy, Responsible AI and Future Readiness

**08:30–15:00**

Facilitate competitive positioning, responsible AI, governance, risk, data, cybersecurity, IP, future telecom and Mpamba scenarios, prototype-to-pilot planning, the Governance Canvas and 90-day commitments.

**Output:** selected pilots, governance responsibilities, action plans, named owners and executive commitments.

## Facilitation Checks

- Protect confidential TNM information during tool use.
- Preserve human accountability in all concepts.
- Test ideas against Malawi's affordability and infrastructure realities.
- Require value measures, risks, safeguards and ownership.
- Capture unresolved questions and decisions at the end of each day.
