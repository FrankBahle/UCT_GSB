**For:** Lead Instructor/Facilitator

## Introduction for Instructors

Welcome! This guide will help you deliver an exceptional three-day AI training experience for SARS employees. The programme is designed to be interactive, practical, and directly relevant to revenue service operations.

### Your Role

As the instructor, you are:
- **Guide:** Help participants navigate their AI learning journey
- **Facilitator:** Enable discovery rather than just lecturing
- **Connector:** Link AI concepts to SARS operations
- **Motivator:** Build confidence and enthusiasm for AI adoption
- **Expert:** Provide deep knowledge when needed

### Teaching Philosophy

This course uses:
- **Adult Learning Principles:** Participants bring valuable experience
- **Constructivism:** Learners build knowledge through experience
- **Hands-On Learning:** Learn by doing, not just listening
- **Contextual Relevance:** Everything ties to SARS context
- **Collaborative Discovery:** Learn together through co-creation

## Facilitation Techniques

### Engagement Strategies

**Every 20 Minutes:** Change the activity
- Lecture → Discussion
- Individual → Group work
- Sitting → Standing
- Listening → Doing

**Energy Management:**
- **High Energy Times:** Morning, after breaks
- **Low Energy Times:** Late morning, mid-afternoon
- **Strategies:** Movement, interaction, energizers, breaks

**Encouraging Participation:**
- Ask open-ended questions
- Use "think-pair-share"
- Call on quiet participants gently
- Validate all contributions
- Build on ideas

**Managing Discussions:**
- Set ground rules early
- Use "parking lot" for off-topic items
- Redirect tangents diplomatically
- Ensure balanced participation
- Summarize key points

### Handling Difficult Situations

**Resistant Participant:**
- Acknowledge concerns
- Ask for specific examples
- Involve in problem-solving
- Speak privately during break if needed

**Dominating Participant:**
- Thank them for contribution
- "Let's hear from others"
- Use structured turn-taking
- Speak privately if persists

**Technical Failure:**
- Stay calm and positive
- Have backup (video, screenshots)
- Use failure as teaching moment
- Keep moving forward

**Group Going Off Track:**
- Acknowledge the topic
- "Let's capture that for later"
- Refocus on objectives
- Adjust if truly important

**Participant Confusion:**
- Check for understanding often
- Use multiple explanations
- Provide examples
- Offer one-on-one during break

## Day-by-Day Teaching Notes

### Day 1: Introduction, Assessment, and Foundations

**Key Focus:** Build foundation and identify needs

**Morning (High Engagement):**
- Set positive tone immediately
- Make personal connections
- Matric Meter assessment is crucial - sets baseline
- Be patient with varying tech skills

**Afternoon (Manage Energy):**
- Pain points workshop is critical for Days 2-3
- Encourage specificity and honesty
- Document everything carefully
- End on positive note about finding solutions

**Common Challenges Day 1:**
- Wide range of AI knowledge levels
  - **Solution:** Pair experienced with beginners
- Skepticism about AI
  - **Solution:** Show relevant examples early
- Fear of job loss
  - **Solution:** Address directly, focus on augmentation

**Teaching Tips Day 1:**
- Use South African examples extensively
- Define jargon and create glossary
- Check understanding frequently
- Be encouraging and patient

---

### Day 2: Practical Demonstrations

**Key Focus:** Show AI in action

**Critical Success Factor:** Demonstrations must work flawlessly
- Test morning of Day 2
- Have video backups
- Practice until you can demo blindfolded

**Pacing:**
- Stick to schedule - easy to run long
- Build in buffer time
- Have "flex" demos you can cut if needed

**Engagement:**
- Let participants try during demos
- Ask predictive questions
- "What would happen if...?"
- Connect back to Day 1 pain points constantly

**Common Challenges Day 2:**
- Demos taking longer than planned
  - **Solution:** Strict timeboxing
- Participants wanting to dive deep on one solution
  - **Solution:** "Let's discuss at break"
- Technical glitches
  - **Solution:** Backup videos, stay calm

**Teaching Tips Day 2:**
- Reference specific participant pain points by name
- Build excitement for Day 3 co-creation
- Take notes on what resonates most
- Adjust Day 3 focus based on interest

---

### Day 3: Co-Creation and Advanced Analytics

**Key Focus:** Collaborative solution design

**Morning Co-Creation:**
- This is THE signature experience
- Your facilitation skills are critical
- Keep group focused and productive
- Ensure everyone contributes
- Manage dominant voices
- Draw out quiet participants

**Afternoon Advanced Analytics:**
- Balance technical depth with accessibility
- Use real data examples
- Connect to co-created solution
- Make it hands-on

**Common Challenges Day 3:**
- Co-creation taking too long
  - **Solution:** Be strict with time boxes
- Group can't agree on problem to solve
  - **Solution:** Vote or facilitator decision
- Solution scope too ambitious
  - **Solution:** Focus on MVP (Minimum Viable Product)
- Advanced analytics too technical
  - **Solution:** Simplify, use analogies

**Teaching Tips Day 3:**
- Celebrate the co-created solution
- Make participants feel ownership
- Connect learning to action
- End on inspirational note

## Demonstration Guidelines

### Preparation for Each Demo

1. **Know Your Data:**
   - Understand what you're showing
   - Know the story it tells
   - Anticipate questions

2. **Smooth Execution:**
   - Practice 3+ times minimum
   - Have all tabs/applications open
   - Clear cache if web-based
   - Test on actual presentation equipment

3. **Narrate While Demonstrating:**
   - Explain what you're doing
   - Why you're doing it
   - What you expect to happen
   - Interpret results

4. **Handle Issues Gracefully:**
   - "Interesting, let's try this instead"
   - Have screenshots as backup
   - Don't waste time troubleshooting live

### Demo Delivery Tips

**Before Starting:**
- Set context: "This tool addresses [pain point X]"
- State what they'll see
- Tell them what to watch for

**During Demo:**
- Slow down - you know it better than they do
- Zoom in if needed for visibility
- Pause for questions at natural points
- Check for understanding

**After Demo:**
- Summarize what was shown
- Highlight key takeaways
- Connect to SARS applications
- Take questions

**Demo Recording:**
- Consider recording successful demos
- Can replace live demo if issues arise
- Useful for participants' future reference

## Interactive Elements

### Quizzes

**Purpose:** Reinforce learning, not test knowledge

**Best Practices:**
- Make them low-stakes and fun
- Review answers immediately
- Use wrong answers as teaching opportunities
- Keep them short (5-10 questions max)
- Consider team-based for engagement

**Tools:**
- Kahoot! (game-based, fun)
- Mentimeter (easy, visual)
- Google Forms (simple, functional)
- Paper (backup if tech fails)

### Workshops

**Your Role:**
- Set clear objectives and instructions
- Provide templates and structure
- Circulate and coach
- Keep time strictly
- Facilitate synthesis

**Common Pitfalls:**
- Instructions unclear
  - **Solution:** Written + verbal + demonstrate
- Groups finish at different times
  - **Solution:** Have extension activities
- Output quality varies
  - **Solution:** Provide examples, coach during

### Group Discussions

**Facilitation Techniques:**
- Start with personal reflection
- Use "think-pair-share"
- Round-robin for equal voices
- Call on specific people: "John, what's your perspective?"
- Summarize themes: "I'm hearing three main ideas..."

**Recording Insights:**
- Assign note-taker
- Use flip charts visible to all
- Take photos of outputs
- Share summaries via email

## Time Management

### Keeping on Schedule

**Essential Practices:**
- Have visible clock/timer
- Build in 5-10 minute buffers
- Know what you can cut if needed
- Warn before ending: "5 more minutes"
- Be disciplined about breaks

**If Running Behind:**
- Prioritize core content
- Cut lower-value activities
- Speed up less critical sections
- Don't rush critical parts
- May extend slightly if group agrees

**If Running Ahead:**
- Don't fill time with fluff
- Go deeper on topic
- More Q&A
- Let people network
- Early break is fine

### Break Management

**Break Times:**
- Morning: 15 minutes
- Lunch: 60 minutes
- Afternoon: 15 minutes

**Managing Breaks:**
- Start and end on time
- Give clear return time
- Remind them twice: "5 minutes to break"
- Use interesting stopping point
- Resume promptly even if some late

## Creating Safe Learning Environment

### Psychological Safety

**Build Trust:**
- Share your own learning journey
- Admit when you don't know
- Make mistakes OK: "Mistakes are learning"
- Encourage questions: "Great question!"
- No such thing as a "dumb question"

**Inclusivity:**
- Use varied examples (gender, age, role)
- Avoid jargon or define it
- Accommodate different learning styles
- Respect diverse perspectives
- Ensure all voices heard

### Handling Sensitive Topics

**Job Displacement Concerns:**
- Acknowledge legitimate fears
- Emphasize augmentation, not replacement
- Focus on new opportunities
- Share positive examples

**Previous Failed Initiatives:**
- Validate past experiences
- Discuss what's different now
- Focus on learning from past
- Build confidence gradually

**Competence Concerns:**
- Normalize learning curve
- Provide support and resources
- Celebrate small wins
- Pair learners with peers
