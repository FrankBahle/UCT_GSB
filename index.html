Programme Positioning

Practical, responsible and future-focused AI leadership for TNM

AI Leadership in Action is an applied, design-led executive intervention tailored to Telekom Networks Malawi and its strategic ecosystem. It equips board members, executives and senior leaders to use artificial intelligence to improve network performance, grow customer value, strengthen Mpamba, enhance enterprise efficiency and govern AI responsibly.

Why This Programme, Why Now?

Five strategic areas where AI can create value for TNM

Artificial intelligence is reshaping telecommunications, mobile financial services, customer engagement, workforce design, enterprise operations, governance and competition.

Strategic Area

Network Intelligence

Predictive maintenance, capacity planning, service assurance, energy optimisation and field-force effectiveness.

Strategic Area

Customer and Commercial Intelligence

Churn prediction, personalisation, next-best action, social listening and customer journey improvement.

Strategic Area

Mpamba and Financial Inclusion

Fraud detection, agent liquidity forecasting, merchant analytics, service reliability and inclusive digital finance.

Strategic Area

Enterprise Productivity

Financial forecasting, HR analytics, legal review, knowledge management, procurement and executive decision support.

Strategic Area

Trust and Governance

Privacy, cybersecurity, regulatory compliance, explainability, human oversight and responsible vendor use.

Three-Day Learning Journey

Diagnose, co-create and move towards pilot-ready action

Participants are positioned as executive co-designers rather than passive learners. Every concept is tested for strategic value, feasibility, responsible governance, inclusion and relevance to Malawi and Africa.

01Day One

Introductions, Shared Understanding & Pain-Point Diagnosis

Begin with people, organisational reality and priority challenges.

  • Executive introductions and co-designer positioning
  • AI foundations and reality game
  • Applied AI demonstration studio
  • TNM and Malawi reality check
  • Pain-point diagnosis and team formation
View Day 1 details
02Day Two

Co-Creation, Practical AI & Rapid Prototyping

Move from priority pain points to visible, testable and responsibly designed AI-enabled concepts.

  • Executive AI toolkit and use-case matching
  • Practical AI demonstration
  • Problem-to-solution architecture
  • Rapid prototyping sprint
  • Prototype walkthroughs and peer critique
View Day 2 details
03Day Three

Strategy, Responsible AI & Future Readiness

Convert promising prototypes into governable strategic choices, pilot pathways and leadership commitments.

  • AI strategy and competitive positioning
  • Responsible AI and human-centred leadership
  • Governance, risk, data, cybersecurity and IP
  • Pilot selection and 90-day action planning
  • Executive governance commitments

Programme-Level Outcomes

What participants will be equipped to do

The programme combines AI foundations, strategic foresight, leadership, responsible AI, governance, data strategy, implementation and executive presentation.

01

Explain AI credibly

Explain foundational AI concepts and distinguish credible applications from hype.

02

Set a TNM AI leadership vision

Articulate an AI leadership vision aligned with TNM strategy and Malawi's socioeconomic realities.

03

Prioritise high-value opportunities

Identify and prioritise AI opportunities across TNM's business and governance functions.

04

Apply responsible AI principles

Address fairness, accountability, transparency, inclusion and accountable human oversight.

05

Build governance and implementation pathways

Establish data, privacy, cybersecurity, intellectual-property, ownership and change considerations.

06

Present a pilot-ready solution

Develop, present and defend an AI-enabled workflow and credible implementation pathway.

Programme Deliverables

Outputs created through the three-day programme

TNM AI Strategic Vision

A concise statement of the desired AI-enabled organisational direction.

AI Maturity Snapshot

A current-state assessment across strategy, data, technology, talent, governance and culture.

Opportunity Portfolio

Prioritised AI opportunities linked to TNM's strategic growth engines.

Responsible AI Concepts

Three to five cross-functional solution concepts with risks and safeguards.

Governance Canvas

Board, executive, data, technical and human-review responsibilities.

Pilot Roadmap and Commitments

A 90-day action plan, named owners and individual and team commitments for follow-through.

Core Strategic Question

Where can AI create a distinctive advantage for TNM that competitors cannot easily copy?

While improving customer value and contributing to Malawi's digital development.
