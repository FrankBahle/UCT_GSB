WELCOME TO AI LEADERSHIP IN ACTION

Purpose of this Brochure
You are warmly welcomed to the Artificial Intelligence Masterclass for the Telekom Networks
Malawi executive cohort. Your facilitator is Professor Abejide Ade-Ibijola, who will guide you
through practical, responsible, and future-focused applications of AI.
This brochure describes the AI Leadership in Action executive programme. It provides the:
1.​ programme philosophy,
2.​ learning outcomes,
3.​ three-day schedule,
4.​ TNM-specific challenge areas,
5.​ group activities, games/simulations,
6.​ delegate profile pages,
7.​ practical canvases, and
8.​ facilitator information.

How to use this brochure​
Participants may use the document before, during and after the programme. The programme
timetable is only one subset of the brochure; the remaining sections support preparation, reflection,
co-creation, implementation planning and follow-through.

Programme Positioning
AI Leadership in Action is an applied, design-led executive intervention tailored to Telekom
Networks Malawi and its strategic ecosystem. It equips board members, executives and senior
leaders to use artificial intelligence to improve network performance, grow customer value,
strengthen Mpamba, enhance enterprise efficiency and govern AI responsibly.
Participants will diagnose:
1.​ TNM’s strategic and operational realities (painpoints),
2.​ co-create priority AI solutions and develop pilot-ready implementation pathways suited to
Malawi’s competitive,
3.​ regulatory and socioeconomic environment.

PROGRAMME CONTEXT AND RATIONALE

Why this Programme, Why Now?
Artificial intelligence is reshaping telecommunications, mobile financial services, customer
engagement, workforce design, enterprise operations, governance and competition.
For TNM, the strategic question is not whether AI will influence the organisation, but how
leadership will direct its use to produce measurable value while preserving trust, accountability and
inclusion.
●​ Network intelligence: predictive maintenance, capacity planning, service assurance, energy
optimisation and field-force effectiveness.
●​ Customer and commercial intelligence: churn prediction, personalisation, next-best action,
social listening and customer journey improvement.
●​ Mpamba and financial inclusion: fraud detection, agent liquidity forecasting, merchant
analytics, service reliability and inclusive digital finance.
●​ Enterprise productivity: financial forecasting, HR analytics, legal review, knowledge
management, procurement and executive decision support.
●​ Trust and governance: privacy, cybersecurity, regulatory compliance, explainability, human
oversight and responsible vendor use.

Malawi Market Realities
●​
●​
●​
●​
●​

Affordability and uneven digital access must inform every AI design decision.
Rural and urban customer realities may require different service models.
Connectivity, cloud costs, data quality and legacy systems can constrain implementation.
Local skills development and responsible partnerships are essential for sustainability.
AI initiatives must strengthen (not weaken) customer trust and regulatory confidence.

Core strategic question​
Where can AI create a distinctive advantage for TNM that competitors cannot easily copy, while
improving customer value and contributing to Malawi’s digital development?

PROGRAMME PHILOSOPHY

Programme Philosophy
This programme is grounded in the belief that artificial intelligence should be approached not
merely as a technical tool, but as a strategic capability for responsible leadership, innovation, and
organisational transformation.
Participants are positioned as executive co-designers, not passive learners. They bring their
operational, technical, commercial, regulatory, and governance experience into the learning
process.
The programme therefore follows these principles:
●​ Begin with organisational reality: Start with TNM’s actual priorities, challenges,
opportunities, and operating context rather than abstract discussions about technology.
●​ Make AI practical and visible: Use demonstrations, examples, and guided activities to show
what AI can realistically do.
●​ Diagnose before designing: Identify pain points, root causes, and user needs before
proposing AI-enabled solutions.
●​ Combine strategy with co-creation: Balance executive-level discussion with practical,
hands-on development of ideas.
●​ Test ideas responsibly: Assess proposed solutions for business value, technical feasibility,
legality, explainability, governance, inclusion, and ethical risk.
●​ Develop pilot-ready concepts: Move beyond broad recommendations towards clearly
defined ideas that can be tested within the organisation.
●​ Create ownership and momentum: Conclude with agreed action steps, accountable owners,
implementation priorities, and institutional learning pathways.
●​ Impact: Think of impact in Malawi, and Broader Africa.
The overall aim is to ensure that participants leave with more than awareness. They should leave
with practical insights, shared priorities, and actionable concepts that can support transformation
within TNM, the wider Malawian economy, and Africa.

LEARNING OUTCOMES

Programme-Level Outcomes
●​ Explain foundational AI concepts and distinguish credible applications from hype.
●​ Assess how AI may change business models, jobs, competition and customer expectations
in Malawi.
●​ Articulate an AI leadership vision aligned with TNM strategy and socioeconomic realities.
●​ Identify and prioritise high-value AI opportunities across TNM’s business and governance
functions.
●​ Apply responsible AI principles covering fairness, accountability, transparency, inclusion
and human oversight.
●​ Develop an AI governance and risk framework suitable for board and executive oversight.
●​ Establish data-governance, privacy, cybersecurity and intellectual-property considerations
for AI projects.
●​ Design AI-enabled workflows that integrate with business processes and preserve
accountable human decision-making.
●​ Build a credible implementation pathway including ownership, data requirements, value
measures and change leadership.
●​ Present and defend a pilot-ready AI solution to an executive audience.
●​ Think Malawi, think Africa.
