# Pain Points Identification Workshop Guide

## Facilitation Guide for Day 1, Session 5

**Duration:** 90 minutes  
**Participants:** SARS employees (all levels)  
**Objective:** Identify and prioritize challenges that AI could help solve

---

## Workshop Overview

This facilitated session helps participants articulate their operational challenges and identify opportunities for AI adoption. The workshop uses structured brainstorming, small group discussions, and prioritization techniques to surface and organize pain points.

---

## Pre-Workshop Preparation (Before Day 1)

### Materials Needed:
- [ ] Flip charts or whiteboards (one per group)
- [ ] Colored sticky notes (3-4 colors)
- [ ] Markers (thick and thin)
- [ ] Voting dots or stickers (5 per participant)
- [ ] Pain points template handouts
- [ ] Digital camera or smartphone for documentation
- [ ] Timer or stopwatch
- [ ] Laptop and projector for synthesis

### Room Setup:
- Arrange tables for groups of 5-7 people
- Ensure adequate space for group work
- Post flip charts around the room
- Have materials easily accessible

---

## Workshop Agenda

### Part 1: Introduction and Context Setting (10 minutes)

**Facilitator Script:**

"Welcome to our pain points identification workshop. Today, we're going to identify the biggest challenges you face in your work at SARS. The goal is not to complain, but to identify opportunities where AI and technology could make your jobs easier and more effective.

**Ground Rules:**
1. All ideas are valid - no judgment
2. Build on each other's thoughts
3. Think big, but be specific
4. Focus on problems, not solutions yet
5. Everyone's voice matters

We'll work through three stages:
- Individual reflection
- Small group discussion
- Whole group synthesis"

**Show Example:**
"For instance, a pain point might be: 'I spend 3 hours daily manually entering data from paper forms, and this delays processing by 2 weeks.'

Notice it's:
- Specific (what task, how much time)
- Measurable (3 hours, 2 weeks)
- Impact-focused (causes delays)

Let's begin!"

---

### Part 2: Individual Reflection (15 minutes)

**Instructions to Participants:**

"Take 15 minutes to reflect individually. Use the provided template or sticky notes. Think about:

**Questions to Consider:**
1. What repetitive tasks consume most of your time?
2. Where do you lack the information you need to make decisions?
3. What processes frustrate you or your customers?
4. Where do errors or delays occur most frequently?
5. What data exists but you can't easily access or analyze?

**For Each Pain Point, Note:**
- What is the problem?
- How often does it occur?
- How much time/resources does it consume?
- Who is affected?
- What is the impact (on you, SARS, taxpayers)?

**Facilitator Actions:**
- Walk around the room
- Offer quiet encouragement
- Answer clarifying questions
- Remind participants of remaining time (5 minutes, 2 minutes)
- Ensure everyone has captured at least 3-5 pain points

---

### Part 3: Small Group Discussion (30 minutes)

**Group Formation:**
Assign participants to groups based on:
- Similar functional areas (recommended), OR
- Mixed cross-functional groups

**Instructions to Groups:**

"Now, share your pain points with your group. As you listen:
1. Look for patterns and themes
2. Ask clarifying questions
3. Add details and examples
4. Group similar pain points together

**Group Tasks (30 minutes):**

**Minutes 1-15: Sharing Round**
- Each person shares their top 3-5 pain points (2-3 minutes each)
- Others listen and take notes
- Ask clarifying questions

**Minutes 16-25: Clustering and Theming**
- Group similar pain points together
- Identify 3-5 major themes
- Give each theme a clear name
- Write on flip chart

**Minutes 26-30: Prioritization**
- As a group, select top 3 pain points
- Consider: frequency, impact, data availability, feasibility
- Be ready to present to larger group

**Facilitator Actions:**
- Circulate among groups
- Keep groups on track with timing
- Help with clustering if groups struggle
- Encourage specificity
- Remind groups to prepare for presentation
- Give 10-minute, 5-minute, and final warnings

**Suggested Clustering Categories:**
- Process inefficiencies
- Data access and analysis challenges
- Customer service issues
- Compliance and enforcement difficulties
- Resource allocation problems
- Technology gaps

---

### Part 4: Group Presentations (30 minutes)

**Format:** Each group presents (5-7 minutes each)

**Presentation Structure:**
1. Introduce your group and functional area
2. Share your 3-5 main themes
3. Highlight your top 3 prioritized pain points
4. Explain why these are priorities
5. Take 1-2 questions

**Facilitator Actions:**
- Keep strict time (give 1-minute warning)
- Capture all pain points on master list
- Note patterns across groups
- Ask clarifying questions
- Thank each group

**Documentation:**
- Assign someone to type all pain points in real-time
- Take photos of flip charts
- Record key quotes

---

### Part 5: Collective Synthesis and Prioritization (15 minutes)

**Activity: Dot Voting**

**Instructions:**
"Now we'll prioritize all pain points collectively. Each of you has 5 voting dots. Place your dots on the pain points you think are:
- Most urgent
- Most impactful if solved
- Most feasible for AI solutions

You can put multiple dots on one item if you feel strongly."

**Steps:**
1. Display all pain points (flip charts on wall or digital projection)
2. Distribute voting dots
3. Allow 5 minutes for voting
4. Tally votes
5. Identify top 10 pain points

**Facilitator Actions:**
- Ensure clarity on all pain points before voting
- Encourage movement around room
- Count votes together
- Highlight top priorities

**Alternative Prioritization Matrix:**

If time allows, plot top pain points on 2x2 matrix:

```
High Impact  |  Quick Wins    |  Major Projects
             |                |
             |----------------|
             |                |
Low Impact   |  Fill Ins      |  Time Sinks
             |                |
             Low Effort       High Effort
```

## Sample Pain Points (for Reference)

### Common Pain Points in Revenue Services:

**Process Inefficiencies:**
- "Manual data entry from tax forms takes 50% of our time"
- "Paper document storage and retrieval is time-consuming"
- "Approval workflows require multiple hand-offs and cause delays"

**Data Challenges:**
- "We have data but can't easily create reports"
- "Can't track trends across years"
- "Data quality issues cause rework"
- "Multiple systems don't talk to each other"

**Customer Service:**
- "Same questions asked repeatedly"
- "Long wait times during peak season"
- "Difficulty accessing taxpayer information quickly"
- "Language barriers with diverse population"

**Compliance & Enforcement:**
- "Can't identify high-risk taxpayers efficiently"
- "Manual audits are resource-intensive"
- "Fraud detection is reactive, not proactive"
- "Limited ability to predict non-compliance"

**Resource Allocation:**
- "Don't know where to focus limited resources"
- "Seasonal spikes overwhelm capacity"
- "Can't quantify impact of different interventions"

## Facilitator Tips

### Creating Safe Space:
- Emphasize this is about improvement, not criticism
- Assure participants their input is valued
- Explain how findings will be used
- Be neutral and non-judgmental

### Managing Dynamics:
- Ensure all voices are heard
- Redirect if discussion becomes unproductive
- Keep energy high with movement and variety
- Acknowledge frustrations while staying constructive

### Keeping Focus:
- Steer away from solutions (that comes later)
- Focus on problems and impacts
- Encourage specificity and examples
- Ask "What else?" to go deeper

### Handling Challenges:
- If group is stuck: provide examples, ask probing questions
- If group dominates: use round-robin or timed turns
- If too negative: acknowledge and redirect to opportunities
- If too abstract: ask for specific examples

**Pain Point #: [Number]**

**Category:** [Process / Data / Customer Service / Compliance / Resource Allocation]

**Description:** [Clear statement of the problem]

**Frequency:** [How often does this occur?]

**Impact:** 
- Time: [Hours/days lost]
- Cost: [Estimated cost if known]
- People Affected: [Number/roles]
- Downstream Effects: [What else is impacted?]

**Current Workaround:** [How is it handled now?]

**Data Available:** [What data exists related to this problem?]

**AI Opportunity:** [Could AI help? How?]

**Priority:** [High / Medium / Low]

**Votes:** [Number from prioritization]

## Sample Workshop Output

### Example: Completed Pain Point

**Pain Point #: 1**

**Category:** Process Inefficiency

**Description:** Compliance officers spend 3-4 hours daily manually extracting data from various systems to create case files for audit investigations. This involves copying information from 5 different systems, checking for inconsistencies, and compiling into a Word document.

**Frequency:** Daily, affects all 50 compliance officers

**Impact:** 
- Time: 150-200 hours per day across team
- Cost: ~R1.2 million annually in staff time
- People Affected: 50 compliance officers
- Downstream Effects: Delays audit starts by 1-2 weeks, limits number of audits possible

**Current Workaround:** Manual process with checklists; quality varies by person

**Data Available:** All source data exists in systems; audit case file structure is standardized

**AI Opportunity:** Automated data extraction and case file generation using AI

**Priority:** HIGH

**Votes:** 23 (highest)

**Potential Solution Category:** Document Processing / Workflow Automation

## Success Criteria

The workshop is successful if:

✓ All participants contribute at least one pain point  
✓ 20-30 distinct pain points are identified  
✓ Pain points are specific and measurable  
✓ Clear priorities emerge from group consensus  
✓ Participants feel heard and engaged  
✓ Output informs Days 2 and 3 content  
✓ At least 2-3 pain points suitable for co-creation
