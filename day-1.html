AI Leadership in Action • 15–17 July 2026

Introductions, Shared Understanding & Pain-Point Diagnosis

Begin with people, organisational reality and priority challenges.

Theme and Focus

Theme: Begin with people, organisational reality and priority challenges.

Focus: Introductions, alignment, AI foundations, demonstrations, TNM reality check, pain-point diagnosis and challenge-team formation.

Day Output

  • Aligned expectations and shared understanding
  • Validated TNM and Malawi organisational realities
  • Prioritised pain points and high-value challenges
  • Cross-functional challenge teams and Day 2 design briefs
Programme Agenda

Day 1 Schedule

TimeSessionActivities
08:30–09:00Welcome, Registration and Executive IntroductionsWarm welcome; facilitator introduction; participant introductions; roles, expectations and perspectives brought into the room.
09:00–09:30Programme Orientation and Co-Designer PositioningProgramme purpose, outcomes, collaboration norms, daily outputs and the role of participants as executive co-designers rather than passive learners.
09:30–10:15AI Reality Game: What AI Is—and What It Is NotInteractive clarification of AI, machine learning, generative AI, predictive AI, automation and agents; separating credible applications from hype.
10:15–10:30Tea / Coffee BreakRefreshments and informal conversation.
10:30–11:30Applied AI Demonstration StudioThe showroom, and a simulation on AI applications in telecommunications, mobile money, customer operations, network management, finance, HR, legal, governance and board reporting.
11:30–12:15TNM and Malawi Reality CheckValidate a working hypothesis of TNM’s current AI readiness, market pressures, infrastructure realities, regulatory context and leadership priorities.
12:15–13:00Leadership Perspectives and Strategic ExpectationsShort executive contributions on operational, commercial, technical, regulatory, workforce and governance expectations for the programme.
13:00–13:45Lunch BreakNetworking and reflection.
13:45–14:45Pain-Point Diagnosis I: Organisational RealityIdentify priority pain points; map affected users, processes, decisions, delays and consequences; distinguish symptoms from root causes.
14:45–15:30Pain-Point Diagnosis II: Prioritisation and ImpactAssess severity, frequency, customer impact, operational cost, risk and strategic importance; select the highest-value challenges.
15:30–16:00Team Formation and Day 1 ClosureForm cross-functional teams; allocate challenge streams; frame Day 2 design briefs; capture reflections and unanswered questions.
